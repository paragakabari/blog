What Types of AI Are Utilized in Gaming?

  • by Petra Games Technology,
  • August 9th, 2024
  • in What Types of AI Are Utilized in Gaming?

In this blog, we will delve into the key aspects of game AI and how it creates seemingly intelligent behaviors in NPCs across various games and franchises. Game AI has progressed from the rudimentary computer-controlled opponents in Pong and early arcade games to the sophisticated NPC agents found in titles like RDR 2, the Halo series, and Bethesda’s Elder Scrolls games. Developers have played a crucial role in defining and redefining game AI, with games such as RDR 2 pushing the boundaries to create NPCs that feel almost lifelike.

What is Game AI?

Artificial intelligence in games, commonly known as game AI, is designed to create seemingly intelligent and responsive behaviors in non-player characters (NPCs), which can include human, humanoid, and animal agents. This technology allows NPCs to act naturally within different game contexts, with human or humanoid characters even performing actions that resemble real human behavior.

Game AI has been a fundamental part of gaming since the arcade era, where it introduced features like difficulty scaling, recognizable enemy movement patterns, and event triggers based on player actions.

Unlike contemporary AI, which relies on machine learning and artificial neural networks, game AI remains distinct. The reason for this difference is that game AI needs to be somewhat predictable. While deep learning AI can become unpredictable as it evolves and learns, game AI is governed by algorithms that ensure players can interact with NPCs in a predictable manner to achieve their in-game objectives.

As game designer and KitFox Games co-founder Tanya Short puts it, game AI is somewhat of a "smoke and mirrors" effect—complex enough to make players believe they are engaging with a responsive intelligence, yet controlled and predictable enough to prevent gameplay from becoming chaotic.

Arcade games were pioneers in using stored patterns to guide enemy movements. The advancement in microprocessor technology introduced greater randomness and variability, exemplified by the classic game Space Invaders (1978). In this game, stored patterns were used to randomize alien movements, ensuring that each playthrough had the potential to be unique. Similarly, in Pac-Man (1980), the distinct movement patterns of the ghosts gave players the impression that each ghost had unique traits, creating the feeling of facing four distinct entities.

Over the years, essential game AI techniques like pathfinding, finite state machines, and behavior trees have played a pivotal role in enhancing game playability and making NPCs more responsive and intelligent. Let's explore these techniques in detail.

How Game AI Makes NPCs Act Intelligently

Game AI employs several key techniques to make non-player characters (NPCs) exhibit intelligent behavior:

Pathfinding:

This technique allows NPCs to navigate the game world efficiently. Algorithms such as A* (A-star) help NPCs determine the optimal route to their destination while avoiding obstacles and adapting to environmental changes. This ensures NPCs move logically and respond dynamically to player actions or changes in their surroundings.

Finite State Machines (FSMs):

FSMs enable NPCs to transition between various states based on specific conditions or events. For instance, an NPC might shift from an idle state to a patrolling state when the game begins, or from a defensive state to an attacking state when detecting a threat. This state-based approach allows NPCs to demonstrate more complex and believable behaviors.

Behavior Trees:

Behavior trees offer a hierarchical and modular method for managing NPC behavior. They consist of nodes representing different actions or decisions, organized in a tree-like structure. This setup enables NPCs to perform complex behaviors by combining simpler actions and decisions flexibly, leading to more nuanced and adaptable responses to player interactions.

Decision-Making Systems:

NPCs use decision-making algorithms to select actions based on their current state, environment, and goals. Techniques such as utility-based systems or weighted decision-making help NPCs assess various options and choose the most suitable action, improving their responsiveness and adaptability.

Learning and Adaptation:

Advanced game AI may incorporate learning mechanisms, such as reinforcement learning, where NPCs enhance their behavior over time through experience. This can result in more intelligent and adaptive NPCs that refine their strategies and responses as they interact with the player and the game world.

By integrating these techniques, game AI creates NPCs that exhibit intelligent and lifelike behaviors, making interactions with them more engaging and immersive.

Cutting-Edge Game AI in Red Dead Redemption 2?

Capturing the full complexity of the in-game AI in Rockstar's Red Dead Redemption 2 (RDR 2) is challenging, but similar to Alien: Isolation, the game showcases a sophisticated integration of multiple AI systems.

In Red Dead Redemption 2, you can engage with every NPC in various ways, and they will respond and comment on your actions. They might notice and remark on details such as blood stains on your shirt, your level of intoxication, or your 'Honor' rating, which reflects your moral choices and influences how the player character, Arthur Morgan, behaves.

In Red Dead Redemption 2 (RDR 2), NPCs exhibit a remarkable degree of realism and interaction. They might tease you about your clothing choices or avoid you if you appear dirty. NPCs follow intricate schedules beyond their basic jobs, and if you shadow them throughout their routine, they might glance nervously over their shoulder and even flee if you persist. Unlike Grand Theft Auto V (GTA V), where attacking an NPC typically results in immediate reactions like fleeing or counter-attacking, NPCs in RDR 2 might first attempt to handle the situation through dialogue, leading to more nuanced interactions.

During combat, NPCs display intelligent behavior: they seek cover, nurse injuries, and may use melee weapons if the opportunity arises. Enemies under attack behave differently from those who are not engaged in combat. When you take refuge in a building, NPCs will systematically cover all exits and coordinate their assault.

The vast wild-west environment of RDR 2 required each NPC to have a distinct personality and mood. Rockstar Games enlisted 1,200 actors from the American Screen Actors Guild, each with an 80-page script, and conducted over 2,200 days of motion-capture sessions to capture realistic mannerisms and behaviors.

The game’s wilderness is home to 200 animal species, each interacting naturally with the environment and other creatures. Herbivores flee from wolves, wolves retreat from grizzly bears, and vultures scavenge abandoned carcasses, reflecting a realistic predator-prey dynamic across multiple ecosystems.

Rockstar also revamped the animation system to create more fluid and accurate human and animal movements. NPCs respond to subtle changes in your facial expressions, posture, and overall demeanor. For example, a well-rested and well-fed Arthur Morgan looks visibly different from one who is exhausted and dirty, and NPCs will take notice of these changes.

Additionally, Rockstar completely redesigned horse animations from the ground up, allowing horse AI to react to player input and movement decisions. This makes the horse an integral part of the gameplay, and a YouTube video even highlights how Rockstar crafted the ‘ultimate video game horse.’

A notable moment occurred when a player discovered an NPC dressed in the exact same outfit as their character. While this might seem unlikely given RDR 2's extensive wardrobe options, the coincidence underscores the game’s complexity. It highlights how numerous and intricately designed NPCs populate the game world, creating a unique and sometimes surprising experience.

In summary, the AI in RDR 2 is exceptionally intricate and continues to deliver unexpected and immersive gameplay experiences, ensuring players will be surprised for years to come.

Conclusion:-

We've explored how game AI can craft intricate interactions, enabling NPCs to exhibit intelligent behavior in context. In the case of Red Dead Redemption 2 (RDR 2), NPCs are so meticulously designed that they seem to have their own rich, complex lives beyond the player’s interactions. It’s apparent that Rockstar didn’t employ neural networks for their game AI, as the integration of cutting-edge AI would likely have been highlighted in their promotional materials. Instead, Rockstar, like other developers, has scaled traditional AI techniques to achieve remarkable results, but game AI might eventually reach a plateau.

Looking ahead, machine learning and neural networks could offer new possibilities for advancing game AI. Imagine an AI system playing millions of games like RDR 2 or Skyrim to fine-tune NPC behaviors while preserving the balance of predictability and randomness required in game AI. Such systems, which thrive on vast datasets, could potentially elevate game AI by learning from extensive gameplay data to refine and enhance NPC behavior.

Developers might harness deep learning AI to analyze and improve game AI techniques, potentially creating more sophisticated and adaptive AI for future games. Titles like Elder Scrolls VI and Rockstar’s next open-world project could significantly benefit from AI-driven advancements in game design. The integration of AI created by AI might push the boundaries of what’s possible in game development, leading to even more immersive and dynamic gaming experiences.
